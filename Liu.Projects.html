Entreprenuership Project(Physical therapy App)

The Entreprenuership Project was a huge end of the year project that was assigned in CSE. The goal of this project was to test our entreprenuership capabilites. We were able to choose partners between all engineering classes, and with them, we had to come up with a "revolutionary" idea that can be easily marketed and appeals to a lot of people. My partners on this project were: Addison Shiu, Harsha Jagarlamudi, Sam Toma. Together, we create PTA(Physical Therapy App), which is an app that allows you to track your physical therapy progress. You can also enter your information such as age, height, and weight, and the current injury. With this informaiton, the app uses its algorithms to calculate what exercises you should do to help improve your current condition. The therapy process also comes with a built in timer to keep track of each of your exercises. When you log out before the whole exercise is done, the app will save what step you were on and what time it was so when you come back to the application, it will continue where you left off. Due to the time restraints, we were not able to accomplish everything we wanted to do such as: a video that describes step by step procedures, more parameters, be able to contact your physical therapist whenever if you need any help. After finishing this project, I was able to learn how to work with a team(though this is the skill that I usually learn), by working as a team, I learned that some projects can't be done with a one person team. I also learned how to listen to others opinions and ideas, and not let their ideas go from one ear to the other. Solar Hydrogen Vehicle Project(11/01/16)

  • Group Project
  • Prompt: Tesla Motors is interested in ways of creating vehicles that are powered by solar or hydrogen fuel cells. They wish to gain some knowledge about the power and speed that it comes with.
  • Task: Create, design, and test a vehicle that is powered by solar and or hydrogen fuel cells.
  • Constraints:
    • Prototype must be constructed with given materials, materials are also very limited
    • Must be able to hold the solar module or hydrogen fuel cell securely and run without tipping the fuel cell, must hold hydrogen fuel cells up with standoffs
    • Top view of vehicle must be no larger than 5”x12”, give or take .1”
    • Needs to be able to easily change between power source configurations for testing purposes
    • Only a 1 motor base, therefore not being very fast or powerful, needs to be scaled up
    • Time limitation, documentation due on 11/17 at 11:59pm

The Solar Hydrogen vehicle project was a huge project that tested how well we worked as a team. This project was assigned in my POE(Principles of Engineering) course during my sophomore year of Dublin High School. We were placed in teams of 3-4(I worked with Alicia Chen, Dhiraj Koppanathi, and Arina Sobol) and were tasked to create a vehicle for tesla that can help them inquire more knowledge about solar and hydrogen fuel cells. We were given VEX materials in order to create a mini vehicle that is powered by one of the cells. Then we were to record the time it took for the vehicle to travel 1 meter on a certain power source. The one major constraint that we came across was the size limits, your vehicle can only be 12 inches by 5 inches. This was an issue since our original design of the motors being directly attached to the wheel would not work. What we did to overcome this obstacle was creating an elevated platform where the motor can power a sprocket which is linked to another sprocket on the front driving wheels by a chain. My main role during this project was to facilitate my team and keep them on track, and to build the actual vehicle. After finishing this project, I was able to better myself as a builder and I grew in my understanding of how vehicles and even VEX functioned. Another very important skill I was able to imporve was to keep calm when situations seemed bleak. The day right before the whole project was due, our vehicle somehow didn't function like normally, it would jitter and was virutally imobile. However, Dhiraj and I both calmed down and were able to fix the issue together as a team. If you would like to reach our documentation of this project please click Here, or on the image above.

POE Project 2.1.4 Aerodynamic design(1/30/17)

  • Group Project
  • Prompt: Nitro Plane is looking for toy projectiles that can be sold as launchers
  • Task: Create, design, and test a projectile that can be launched from a launcher
  • Constraints:
    • Prototype must be constructed with paper materials
    • Body Diameter must fit over 3/4" PVC tube
    • Body Length between 10"-15"
    • Wingspan no greater than 24"
    • Launched at a Specific PSI
    • Final Launch day (1/23/17) documentation due date (1/30/17)

The Aerodynamic design project was a huge project that evaluated how well we worked with a team on a physical design that was tested and comapred with others. This project was assined in my POE(Principles of Engineering) course during my sophomore year of Dublin High School. In this project, we were allowed to select our own team and I chose Uday Kalra and Nicholas Talin. In this project, we were tasked to create a projectile that can be launched with the given launchers. The way the projectiles were evaluated was the distance it traveled vs the amount of time it took to land. The projectiles were made with paper that was rolled around a 3/4" PVC pipe which allowed the projectile to fit onto the launcher. The launcher was made from a bike bump that increased pressure on a reservoir and is released to propell the projectile forward. The first design I made was very simple, it was a 12" tube that had wings at the end of it. It faired quite well, going roughly 45 yards, however, compared to the other projectiles, it wasn't up to par. Then I began to lighten up the body of the projectile and slightly increase the weight of the tip. I also reinforced the wings to increase stability since the previous test was very unstable. I teamed up with Andrew Hoffman and we set out to build a game of hide and seek. This game requires 2 players, one hides and one seeks. DE Project 2.3.5 PLD Design

  • Individual Project
  • Problem Statement: Mr. Kermit has had a lot of intruders on his land and needed a sign that can deter any evil.
  • Task: Design a phrase that is displayed on a seven segment display that fits Mr. Kermit’s needs.
  • Constraints:
    • 16 letter word/phrase.
    • Has to be built in a PLD.
    • No more than 5 spaces.
  • Deliverables:
    • A simulation of the circuit on Multisim.
    • A complete breadboard of the circuit.
    • A complete documentation of project.
    • An Oral presentation.

Project 235 was a project assigned in my digital electronics class at Dublin High School. This project was assigned right after I learned about a programmable logic device (PLD) which is a device that allows the engineer to program and store complex logic (that would normally take hundreds of wires) into this 2-3 inch device. In this project, I was tasked to use the PLD to create a banner that is 16 letters in length. The PLD cycles through 16 different logics gates that return different outputs, each corresponding to a specific segment on the Mydaq's built-in seven segment display. My breadboard cycled through each of the 16 characters and spelled out "Hippity Hoppity". The 2 most challenging parts of this project were: (1) correctly linking each logic condition for every letter with the corresponding segment and (2) finding the perfect clock frequency so that the letters are cycling at the perfect pace.
